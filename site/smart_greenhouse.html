I. Abstract

In this academic report, we will focus on the IoT-powered greenhouse system and the benefits of incorporating different networking mechanism in greenhouse farming. We will analyze the system architectures as a whole the role of sensors and actuators in particular, and its impacts on energy conservation and cost reduction. Furthermore, we will explore deeply the implementation of the cloudbased technology on plant growth and the overall management of the greenhouse activities. Ultimately, this report suggests some practical approaches to deploy such systems in the Seneca King Smart Greenhouse initiative.

II. Introduction

The world today is facing with the myriads of global issues. One of them, the population explosion, especially in developing countries, become a threat to the food supply security. In addition, traditional agricultural method seems vulnerable when confronting unprecedented weathers that caused by climate change.

The rise of technology has led to an increase in innovative and sustainable farming methods, with smart systems at the forefront. One such system is the Cloud-based IoT greenhouse model, which utilizes various technologies such as cloud services and automation that not only provides a controlled climate for effective plant growth but manage and optimize the farming process remotely and effectively.

III. Cloud-based System for Smart Greenhouse

3.1. Overview of the System

IoT network including sensors, actuators, embedded controllers, with the integration of cloud service and a dedicated app will give the farmers more power to manage their greenhouse remotely and effectively, to harvest better crops all year. In the nutshell, the real time data collected from sensors and cameras at the farm are sent to the cloud server to save in a data warehouse and process for analytic tasks. Users can easily manage all the systems operating in the greenhouse via an app available across devices (laptop/phones). It will assist farmers in making faster and better decision on managing the greenhouse activities and plants development.

The app offers a main dashboard for users to track the essential environmental parameters such as soil moisture, light, temperature, air humidity, and CO2 emission level for any particular area the greenhouse along with the information of specific plants. With the help of machine learning algorithm, it can predict the possibilities of disease development on a plant and recommend steps that should be taken in an hour-basis. Eventually, the final decision will be sent to the executing devices via network and will be carried out automatically by actuators (such as turning on/off vent, cooling, heating, irrigation systems, etc.) to maintain the optimal growing conditions for the plants.

3.2. Tracking and Gathering Real-time Data

3.2.1 Enivronmental sensors

Monitoring the environmental factors is critical to the growth of a crop. This becomes a huge advantage for a plant being grown in the greenhouse where all climatic parameters can be controlled and adjusted precisely customized for each specific plant type. It avoids unpredicted natural weather conditions that will threat the crop while allowing plants to grow faster, all year round. There are a number of parameters important that needs great supervision such as temperature, air humidity, soil moisture, nutrient level, CO2 level.

Each type of sensor requires different installation approaches. For example, a temperature sensor should be placed at various heights within the greenhouse as temperature can vary greatly at different heights. This method is suitable for vertical farming where layers of plants are stacked vertically. Moreover, temperature sensors are also put near vents or heaters to monitor their effectiveness. Humidity sensors, on the other hands, are positioned near plants that requires specific humidity. Keep in mind not to place them near the water sources such as irrigation systems as this can lead to inaccurate readings. Nutrition sensors can be installed near the root zone where the majority of the roots are located. All sensors should be dispersed into strategic places to precisely collect the climatic parameter in different areas of the greenhouse.

3.2.2. Camera System

Images and videos are the other fruitful data sources that can be collected to better monitor the growth of plants. A camera system is suggested to gather video timestamp and high-resolution images to detect disease that would probably develop on plants. Images are then processed to remove any noise or artifacts that may intervene the analysis. This can involve techniques such as filtering, smoothing, and color protection. Features that are relevant to disease detection such as color, texture, shape and size information are extracted from the images. Finally, a classification algorithm will be used to recognize patterns and features associated with known disease labels. The power of image processing together with the computer vision technology help timely identify locations and type of disease that is likely to develop on a plant. “According to a study, the system used KNN cluster algorithm demonstrated high efficiency in both monitoring plant growth and detecting diseases, 1.71 times faster than other normal methods.
